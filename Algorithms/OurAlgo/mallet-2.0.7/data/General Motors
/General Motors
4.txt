Unredacted copy of internal General Motors report on its failure to disclose deadly ignition switch defect in small cars shows that death of Jean P Averill in 2003 looms large in crisis that has engulfed GM; Averill, among 13 victims that GM's legal department linked to defect, was earliest such fatality company logged on its books; Averill's family, until informed by The New York Times, was not told of link by GM and did not know it was eligible to receive a minimum of $1 million from a victim's compensation fund the automaker established.
